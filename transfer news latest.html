Alaba to lead Pep's Man City rebuild

Manager planning overhaul to compensate loss of key players

David Alaba is one of Pep Guardiola's targets to lead a new-look Manchester City, according to the Guardian.

The Bayern Munich star is equally proficient at left-back or in the middle of defence, where he has shifted in Germany due to the emergence of Alphonso Davies.

And with both Leroy Sane and David Silva leaving at the end of the season, plenty of further new faces could be arriving at the Etihad Stadium in the weeks to come. 

Napoli make €31m Cengiz Under offer

7/2/2020

Napoli have made a move to sign Roma forward Cengiz Under, tabling a bid of up to €31 million (£28m/$35m), Il Messaggero reports.

Paulo Fonseca's side are apparently open to selling the Turkey international, although are demanding a figure of €35m (£32m/$39m) to get the deal over the line.

Under is interested in making the switch to Napoli, with Roma also currently working on a deal to sign Manchester United centre-back Chris Smalling on permanent basis.

Borussia Dortmund are ready to rival Manchester United in the race for Aston Villa playmaker Jack Grealish, according to the Transfer Window Podcast.

The Red Devils have been leading that chase, in a search for more creativity, but Bundesliga giants are preparing to make their own move.

Real Madrid can't afford to move for Mane

Real Madrid cannot afford to make a move for Liverpool forward Sadio Mane, according to the Daily Mail.

Zinedine Zidane is a big fan of the Senegalese forward, but the Blancos have seen their spending power reined in and need to sell in order to free up funds.

Sane arrives for Bayern medical

Man City star all set to leave
Manchester City winger Leroy Sane has arrived in Munich to undergo the first part of his medical, Sky Sport Germany reports.
Pep Guardiola has already confirmed that the Germany international is set to leave City at the end of the 2019-20 season, despite him having wanted the 24-year-old to stay put. But images of the former Schalke star arriving in Munich have now surfaced, with his switch to the Allianz Arena inching ever closer.

Angel Gomes eyes move abroad

Angel Gomes is now weighing up his next move and is eyeing a switch abroad after leaving Manchester United, the Manchester Evening News reports.

The midfielder is now a free agent after his contract at Old Trafford expired on June 30 although is apparently yet to settle on a new club.
Teams from Spain, Germany, France, and Portugal have reportedly expressed an interest in signing Gomes, although, despite recent press reports, it would appear he's not heading to Chelsea.

Mourinho wouldn't swap Lo Celso for Fernandes

Tottenham head coach Jose Mourinho has claimed that he would not swap Giovani Lo Celso for Bruno Fernandes if given the chance, with the Manchester United midfielder having reportedly been on the verge of signing for Spurs in the past.
"I don't know anything about that," Mourinho told Sky Sports when asked about Spurs' reported talks with Sporting.
"But if that is true, and Giovani Lo Celso was the player that came to Spurs [instead], then I would say I wouldn't change Lo Celso for any player. Not just for Bruno, but for any player."

Everton to sign Nkounkou on free transfer

Everton will sign teenage defender Niels Nkounkou after rejecting the offer of a new contract with Olympique Marseille, according to The Times.

A left-back with the ability to play in midfield, it is reported that the 19-year-old will train with the first team but first play for the Toffees' Under-23 side.

Messi return to Newell's 'not impossible'

Newell's Old Boys vice-president Cristian D'Amico has reiterated that the doors are open for Lionel Messi, while conceding that the player will have the last word on whether he wishes to return.
Messi, who played as a boy in the Newell's youth set-up before moving to Catalunya at the age of 13, once more struggled to inspire his under-performing Barcelona side as the Blaugrana were held 2-2 by Atletico Madrid on Tuesday to record their third draw in their last four outings in La Liga.
That run has seen them surrender the title initiative to rivals Real Madrid, who can move four points clear at the top with victory over Getafe on Thursday. 

Aubameyang still undecided on new Arsenal contract

Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang admitted that he is yet to reach a decision on extending his Arsenal stay, but was happy to see Bukayo Saka sign his own deal.
Aubameyang struck with two goals as the Gunners breezed past Norwich City 4-0 on Wednesday, a result that moves Mikel Arteta's charges up to seventh, six points shy of Manchester United in fifth; while deepening the Canaries' relegation woes at the foot of the Premier League table.
The Gabon international is now level with Jamie Vardy on 19 goals as the league's top scorer in 2019-20 and also broke the Premier League record as the quickest Arsenal footballer to reach his half-century. 

Guardiola rules out signing Sancho

Pep Guardiola has ruled out signing former Manchester City winger Jadon Sancho as a possible replacement for Leroy Sane.
Sancho, 20, has been linked with a move away from Borussia Dortmund in the transfer window and the Premier League is seen as a likely destination with Manchester United and Chelsea potential options.
City are now in the market for a new winger with Sane close to completing a move to Bayern Munich for an initial €49 million (£45m/$55m) with another €11m (£10m/$12m) in add-ons.
