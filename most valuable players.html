The 20 most valuable players in world football right now have been revealed (20-1)

20 | Frenkie de Jong | €102.1m

Barca signed De Jong for €75 million last summer and the 23-year-old is already impressing at the Camp Nou. Will only get better - and more valuable.


19 | Mason Mount | €102.5m

This season was a breakthrough season for Mount at Chelsea. Playing under Frank Lampard, the 21-year-old has the perfect tutor.


18 | Matthijs de Ligt | €104.7m

De Ligt joined Juventus last summer in a deal worth around €85 million. Still only 20-years-old and the potential to be the best defender in the world.


17 | Bruno Fernandes | €104.9m

Fernandes’ value has risen since moving to Manchester United for €80 million in January. He’s been brilliant since arriving in England.


16 | Serge Gnabry | €106.8m

The former Arsenal man has scored 19 goals this season - six of which came against Tottenham and Chelsea in two matches.


15 | Erling Haaland | €107.3m

Just the 41 goals and counting for Haaland this season for Red Bull Salzburg and Borussia Dortmund. Ten goals in eight Champions League appearances shows the 19-year-old can really do it at the highest level.


14 | Joao Felix | €107.9m

Felix’s valuation is actually lower than the €126 million Atletico Madrid paid for him last summer.

Four goals in 20 appearances this season hasn’t been a great return but he’s still only 20-years-old.



13 | Gabriel Jesus | €113.1m

The Brazilian often has to play second fiddle to Sergio Aguero at Manchester City but he’s still managed 63 goals in 138 appearances for City.


12 | Bernardo Silva | €115m 

Bernardo looks set to be given an even more prominent role at City when David Silva leaves. A crucial player in Pep Guardiola’s side.


11 | Roberto Firmino | €118.5m

Talking of crucial players in systems. Firmino makes Jurgen Klopp’s side tick even if he doesn’t have the greatest goalscoring record.


10 | Harry Kane | €118.7m

If he stays in England, Kane could trouble Alan Shearer’s Premier League goal record. He’s notched 136 in 198 appearances in the league and is still only 26.


9 | Alphonso Davies | €133.5m

Davies has been a revelation for Bayern Munich this season and is quite possibly the quickest player in the world right now. Bayern signed the 19-year-old from the MLS in 2018 for around €18 million.


8 | Antoine Griezmann | €136.4m

Signed by Barca for his release clause, €120 million, last summer. He’s managed eight goals during his debut season.


7 | Sadio Mane | €139.2m

Some Liverpool fans will say Mane is more valuable than Salah - but not CIES. Mane shared the Premier League Golden Boot last season and already has 14 league goals to his name this campaign.


6 | Mohammed Salah | €144.9m

But Salah is worth around €5 million more than Mane. Since signing for Liverpool for €50m, the Egyptian has scored 91 goals in 144 appearances. Incredible.


5 | Marcus Rashford | €152.3m

Rashford is the fifth most expensive player in the world right now. At the tender age of 22, the Manchester United striker has already played more than 200 times for the club, scoring 64 goals.


4 | Trent Alexander-Arnold | €171.1m

European, world and soon to be English champion, 21-year-old Alexander-Arnold has established himself as the best right-back in the world. He’s already played 125 times for his boyhood club and look as though he’s only going to improve.


3 | Jadon Sancho | €179.1m

The 20-year-old Englishman is seriously impressing in Germany with Dortmund. This season he’s already hit the 20 goal mark and has been linked with a big-money move back to the Premier League.


2 | Raheem Sterling | €194.7m

Sterling may not be having the greatest 2019/20 season but he’s taken his game to new levels at Manchester City. Since arriving in 2015, he’s scored 89 goals in 230 appearances and is capable of playing for any club in the world.


1 | Kylian Mbappe | €259.2m

The most valuable player in the world is, obviously, Mbappe. 21-years-old, the second most expensive player in the world, a world champion, a four-time Ligue 1 winner and 121 goals scored in 191 appearances. Surely he’s guaranteed to win a couple of Ballon d’Ors in the coming years.
