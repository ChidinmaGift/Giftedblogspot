Champions League to continue away from public scrutiny in August

The Champions League, Europe's top football club rivalry, will continue away from public scrutiny one month from now in the wake of being stopped in March due to the coronavirus pandemic, with all the quarter-finals being played in Portugal's capital, Lisbon.  

Real Madrid could meet Juventus, while Bayern Munich will confront Barcelona or Napoli in the quarter-finals in Lisbon.

UEFA, European football's administering body, held the draw for the one-leg quarter-finals and semi-finals at their base camp in Nyon, Switzerland, on Friday.

Four quarter-finalists were affirmed before the COVID-19 pandemic suspended the season, while the staying four most recent 16 second legs will be played at the particular clubs' home arenas.

Spanish mammoths Real Madrid could meet Italian side Juventus of its previous legend Cristiano Ronaldo, while German association pioneers Bayern Munich may confront Barcelona.

Genuine should initially finish up their last-16 bind with Manchester City. They enter the second, away, leg trailing 2-1, having lost at home.

Juventus, in the interim, must conquer a 1-0 first-leg rout by Olympique Lyonnais in the last-16 phase when they play again in Turin.

Friday's draw additionally matched competition debutants Atalanta with Paris Saint-Germain and RB Leipzig with Atletico Madrid.

The quarter-finals and semi-finals will be played as irregular take out matches as opposed to two-legged ties as typical. The last will be played on August 23.

Here is the finished timetable of matches:

Quarterfinal draw (August 12-15)

QF 1: Real Madrid or Manchester City v Olympique Lyonnais or Juventus

QF 2: RB Leipzig v Atletico Madrid

QF 3: Napoli or Barcelona v Chelsea or Bayern Munich

QF 4: Atalanta v Paris St Germain

Semi-last draw (August 18-19)

S1: Winners of QF 1 v Winners of QF 3

S2: Winners of QF 2 v Winners of QF 4

Last (August 23)

Champs of S1 v S2
