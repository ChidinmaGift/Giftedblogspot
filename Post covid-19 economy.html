POST COVID-19 Economy

By Gifted posted 29 April 2020

Although the ongoing crisis in the local and global markets which has been triggered by the deadly virus, Covid-19 pandemic and unprecedented dive in oil prices, the telecommunications industry has been topped to balance the economy, post-COVID.

In the first two months since the global lockdown went into stay , more than 24,000 jobs have been cut in Nigeria. The Nigerian travel industry is said to have lost not less than N180 billion and thousands of jobs.

The country's well known economic mainstay, the crude oil, which was predicated on 57 dollars per barrel for the 2020 budget has dropped to below 30 dollars per barrel.

 

The International monetary fund, IMF has predicted that there may be a global recession after the pandemic. With these current available statistics, there's no dispute, Nigeria's economy may likely hit worst after the pandemic.

Healthy telecom sector

Nevertheless , Nairametrics Financial Advocates, a leading financial resource company, with special focal point on financial literacy, investor advocacy and business intelligence, says the telecom sector is projected to stay more healthier than most other industries that have received heavier blows from the crash of the deadly virus

In spite of the fact that, Analysys Mason tipped that global telecoms operators may likely suffer a decline of 3.4 per cent, equivalent to $40 billion in revenue in 2020 due to confrontations originating from the deadly virus Covid-19 pandemic, Nairametrics, still sees the telecoms sector as a dazzling spot amidst the despondency .

Its conviction that the telecom sector will stay buoyant after the pandemic is on three factors- investments made pre-COVID, social media culture and data growths. Another is proactive reactions of some tech firms in outlining on the stock exchange.

 

Nairametric said: "We see that Nigeria's key telecommunications companies have invested laboriously in internet infrastructure in a bid to upgrade 4GLTE coverage beyond the country. Furthermore, increased competition among the providers has forced bundle prices lower, making internet usage very captivating to the average Nigerian.

In addition, multiplying smartphone penetration, multiplying digitisation of the Nigerian economy and a fledgling social media culture are also among many other factors driving internet penetration within the country".


It added that "all in all the COVID-19 pandemic ravaging the country which has brought about movement restrictions in many states and with key population centres like Lagos and Abuja seeing its citizens forced to stay at home, we expect a surge in data usage among the populace.We consequently expect better numbers from the telecom companies in the months ahead. In light of this, we see key telecom stocks like MTNN and Airtel Africa as good defensive stocks in this pandemic.

A defensive stock is a stock that provides steady dividends and fixed earnings regardless of the state of the overall stock market.

Investors' favourite stocks

Since the outlining of its 20.35 billion share units worth N1.8 trillion on the Nigerian Stock Exchange (NSE), MTN Nigeria's stock has been narrated as one of investors' favourites at the Exchange.

Outlining on the NSE premium board on May 16, 2019, as the first telco to do so, MTNN is said to have maintained second most capitalised stock.

 


The board is known an outlining segment for the elite group of issuers that meet the Exchange's most stringent corporate governance and listing standards.

Accordingly, the telco immediately, joined other elite stocks such as Dangote Cement Plc, FBN Holdings Plc, Zenith International Bank Plc, Access Bank Plc, Lafarge Africa Plc, Seplat Petroleum Development Company Plc and United Bank for Africa Plc.

Recall that at outlining , Chief Executive Officer of the NSE, Oscar Onyema, had described MTN's listing as a promising development in the country's telecommunications sector and capable of encouraging other players in the sector to explore the different opportunities in the capital markets for raising long term capital.

He said: "As an outlining platform of choice, today's outlining will add to our bouquet of diverse investment offerings to the public. Having MTN Nigeria outlined in our market is a testament of The Exchange's commitment to building a dynamic and inclusive market and creating channels for sustainable investment.

"This outlining will promote liquidity for MTN Nigeria, enable its value and increase transparency, as our platform remains one of the best avenues for raising capital and enabling sustainable growth for national development," he said.

Danbatta's foresight

Meanwhile, the irony of the telco's presence in the domestic capital market is that it was not on its volition but as part of obligation mandated on it by the Nigerian Communications Commission (NCC), as atonement for SIM card registration infractions.

However, after a very long negotiation with both the regulatory agency and the FederalGovernment, the company had the fine reduced to N330bn.

Public outlining was one of the conditions arrived at with the telecommunications company before the Federal Government agreed to decrease the penalty to N330bn.

Telecom experts have described NCC's role in MTN's listing as unprecedented and creative; exploiting the then controversial situation to create investment opportunities for local and foreign investing communities.

Seeing how stocks of numerated telecoms companies are creating values for capital gains and wealth creation in the market, they are encouraging the Commission to make outlining on NSE a precondition for giving out operating license to players in the telecoms industry.

A telecom engineer, Basil Nwanokwuru told Hi-Tech that "hearing that the telecommunications companies outlined in the Nigerian stock exchange are touted to have the power of providing stability to the entire economy after COVID-19 is cheery news.

This is to say that the foresight Prof Danbatta displayed in exploiting the moment of MTN's infraction to ask them to list is yielding dividends. Now, we need to take it a step further, by making it a precondition for issuing a new telecom license in this country" he included.
